# Design Guidelines — FarmApp (Swine CRM)

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** Small-to-mid commercial piggery operators in the Philippines and their on-farm teams. Multi-user farms with four distinct roles — Owner (often off-site, manages from phone), Manager (on-site supervisor), Worker (farrowing attendant, feeder, general farm hand — performs most daily logging), Veterinarian (visiting, writes health records only).

**Context of use:**
- One-handed phone use in a barn — gloves, low light, occasional dust.
- Rural Philippines connectivity: 4G that drops, prepaid data, low-end Android devices common alongside iOS.
- Workflow happens during shifts — quick logging between physical animal handling tasks, not deep deskwork sessions.
- Owner/Manager sometimes review on tablets or larger phones; Workers almost always on phones.

**Job to be done:** Replace paper barnsheets with a fast, accurate, team-visible record of every pig's lifecycle (breeding → farrowing → health → sale/mortality) and every team action, so the owner can see what happened today without being there.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** **Calm. Grounded. Trustworthy.**

**Voice & tone:** Plain language, no buzzwords. Numbers and dates over decorations. Confident in the boring stuff (capacities, dates, counts) — the app's job is to remove uncertainty, not to entertain. Errors and confirmations are direct ("Mark deceased? This cannot be undone.") rather than soft or apologetic.

**Emotional goals:** Confidence (the app won't lose my data), control (I can see what's happening on my farm right now), respect (this isn't condescending or gamified).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Calm, structured, content-first. Generous whitespace; large tap targets; one or two strong colors carrying weight, not six competing for attention. Cards that feel like physical index cards on a barnsheet — slightly elevated, rounded, neutral surfaces — not glass, not gradient, not glossy.

**Theme:** **Light only** for v1. Primary green (`#2E7D32`) anchors the brand; dark accent green (`#1A3A3A`) for high-emphasis buttons; near-black text on warm-grey background (`#F5F5F5`).

**Reference (positive):** [EveryPig](https://www.everypig.com/) — explicit inspiration. Their "made for people who hate technology" stance, communication-centric Farmfeed pattern (already mirrored in our Activity Feed), and content-over-chrome surfaces are the north star.

**Anti-references (explicitly avoid):**
- **Enterprise SaaS feel** — no Salesforce/SAP-style data density, no spreadsheet grids as primary UI, no jargon-laden labels.
- **Consumer-app gamification** — no badges, streaks, points, confetti, mascots, or pseudo-game progression. Logging mortality is not a "win."
- **Generic Material 3 defaults** — the app should look intentional, not "I just `flutter create`d this." Distinctive type ramp, considered card shadow, custom empty states.
- **Heavy decoration** — no gradients, glassmorphism, neumorphism, drop-shadows on text, ornate borders, illustrated mascots. Calm wins.

**Cross-platform approach:** **Material 3 everywhere + iOS niceties.** Single visual language across Android and iOS for predictability and maintainability. Layer in iOS-native touches where they reduce friction: swipe-back gestures via `CupertinoPageRoute` (or `PageTransitionsTheme` with `CupertinoPageTransitionsBuilder` on iOS), Cupertino-style date and time pickers via `showCupertinoModalPopup` on iOS, light haptic feedback on destructive confirms, iOS-style scrolling physics (`ScrollPhysics.bouncing` on iOS, clamping on Android). Status bar matches scaffold background — no awkward dark bar against light content.

### Design Principles

These five principles win every micro-decision:

1. **Content over chrome.** If a card border, divider, or shadow doesn't help the user read or act on data, remove it. Every pixel of decoration costs cognitive load that workers in a barn cannot spare.

2. **Big targets, generous breathing room.** Minimum 48×48 dp tap targets (gloves), minimum 16 dp gutters, 24 dp between content groups. Crowding reads as enterprise SaaS — exactly what we're avoiding.

3. **One accent at a time.** Each screen has one dominant action and one dominant data point. Multiple competing CTAs, badges, or status colors at equal weight is a smell. Status pills earn red sparingly (mortality, needs-repair, overdue) so users learn to scan for them.

4. **Numbers are first-class citizens.** This is a record-keeping app — counts, dates, weights, durations are the actual product. Use a tabular numeric font feature where supported (`fontFeatures: [FontFeature.tabularFigures()]`), align decimals, give numbers more visual weight than their labels.

5. **Predictable across iOS and Android.** A worker who learns the app on Android should not be confused on iOS, and vice versa. We adopt platform conventions where they reduce friction (back gesture, date picker style) and ignore them where they fragment learning (we don't switch entire layouts or icon families).

### Spacing & Layout System

Use a strict 4 dp baseline grid. Approved spacing values: **4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48 dp.** Avoid 6, 10, 14, 20, 28 — they look arbitrary and stack badly.

Page gutters: **16 dp** on phones, **24 dp** on tablets (≥600 dp wide).
Card internal padding: **16 dp.**
Between content groups in a ListView: **16 dp.**
Between sections (headings): **24 dp.**

### Typography

**Family:** Poppins (already in pubspec). Resist adding a second display face — Poppins handles both body and display roles via weight contrast.

**Type ramp** (all weights are `FontWeight.w500` minimum for headlines for warmth):
- `headlineLarge` 28 / w700 / -0.5 letter spacing — page titles only
- `headlineMedium` 22 / w600 / -0.25 — card headers, section titles
- `headlineSmall` 18 / w600 / 0 — sub-headers in cards
- `titleMedium` 16 / w600 — list-item primary text, prominent stats
- `bodyLarge` 16 / w400 — primary body
- `bodyMedium` 14 / w400 — secondary body, subtitles
- `labelLarge` 14 / w600 — button text, chip text
- `labelMedium` 12 / w500 — overlines, tiny labels
- Numbers use `tabularFigures` so 12 / 100 / 1,250 align as columns.

### Color System

**Single accent strategy:** the brand green carries weight; every other color is functional.

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `primary` | `#2E7D32` | Primary buttons, primary chips, active selection, brand mark |
| `primaryContainer` | `#C8E6C9` | Subtle backgrounds for "good" state highlights (today's tasks card, roster) |
| `surfaceTint` | derived from primary | M3 elevation overlay |
| `surface` | `#FFFFFF` | Cards |
| `surfaceContainer` | `#F1F3F5` | Page background (subtle warmth — not pure grey) |
| `surfaceContainerHigh` | `#E9ECEF` | Hover/pressed surfaces, dividers when needed |
| `outline` | `#D4D6D8` | Borders, dividers (used rarely — prefer whitespace) |
| `onSurface` | `#1B1F1A` | Body text (very dark green-leaning, not pure black) |
| `onSurfaceVariant` | `#5A625C` | Secondary text |
| `error` | `#C0392B` | Mortality, needs-repair, overdue tasks, destructive confirms |
| `tertiary` | `#E8A317` | Warning state — "needs attention" but not destructive (e.g., approaching task deadline) |

The previous theme's `#FFC107` accent yellow is dropped — too saturated. New tertiary `#E8A317` reads as concern, not celebration.

### Component Conventions

- **Cards**: 16 dp radius, elevation 1 (M3 surfaceTint level 1), 16 dp internal padding, 12 dp vertical margin in ListView contexts.
- **Buttons**: Pill (28 dp radius) for primary CTAs, 8 dp radius for in-card secondary actions. Always at least 48 dp tall.
- **Inputs**: Filled style (`InputDecorationTheme(filled: true, fillColor: surfaceContainer)`), no border by default, 12 dp radius, 16 dp internal padding. Label above the field, not floating, for readability with gloves.
- **Chips**: 32 dp tall, 8 dp horizontal padding, `labelMedium` text. Selected = filled with primary; unselected = outlined.
- **Status pills**: Earn red sparingly. Equipment "in use" = primary green, "available" = neutral outline, "needs repair" = error red. Same scheme for pig status, task overdue, mortality flags.
- **Icons**: Continue using `iconsax` for consistency. Outlined style throughout. 20 dp default size, 24 dp for primary actions.
- **Photos**: 12 dp rounded corners always, no shadow on photos (the photo itself is the focal point).
- **Dividers**: Use sparingly. Whitespace is preferred over a line. When needed, use `outlineVariant` (very low contrast).

### Motion

Restrained but present — every state change gets a quick animation so the user trusts that the system responded.

- Page transitions: `CupertinoPageTransitionsBuilder` on iOS, `ZoomPageTransitionsBuilder` on Android.
- Tap feedback: M3 ink ripple on Android, scale (0.97) + haptic light impact on iOS for primary buttons.
- List item updates: 200 ms fade for new items, 150 ms for removed.
- Pull-to-refresh: standard platform indicator (`RefreshIndicator` on Android, Cupertino on iOS via adaptive).
- Activity feed: new entries slide-in from top with a brief subtle highlight.
- **No springs, no parallax, no scroll-driven shaders.** Calm wins.

### Accessibility

Even though no specific accessibility requirements were called out, the user context (workers in barns, low light, gloves) naturally pushes us toward strong defaults:

- WCAG AA contrast minimum for all text on its background. Verify `onSurface` on `surfaceContainer` and pill text on each status color.
- Minimum 48 dp tap targets — already a design principle for gloves.
- Respect `MediaQuery.textScaleFactor` — let users with older eyes scale up text.
- Respect `MediaQuery.disableAnimations` — skip non-essential motion.
- VoiceOver / TalkBack labels on icon-only buttons (status chips, FABs).
- High-traffic flows (add pig, log breeding, log mortality) should be navigable without precise gestures — large hit areas, generous spacing between adjacent actions.

### What we do NOT add in v1

To stay focused and avoid scope creep on the visual pass:

- No custom illustrations, mascots, or hero images.
- No animated splash screen beyond a calm static logo.
- No theme-toggle UI (light only).
- No localization framework yet (Sub-project C).
- No accessibility audit beyond the defaults above (would be its own pass).
